The 52-Feature Overfitting Trap & Model Evolution

In Run 7, we engineered and tested a massive 52-feature vector (incorporating 37 new features from librosa, including MFCCs, spectral rolloff, and bandwidth)[cite: 1]. The goal was to capture advanced phonetic transitions. However, this experiment revealed a classic machine learning trap: The CV Illusion.

English Delay Across Runs (ms)

The CV Illusion: 16-Feat vs. 52-Feat

During cross-validation (CV), the 52-feature model appeared to drop the delay to an incredible 892ms[cite: 1]. However, applying 52 complex features to only ~496 total training pauses led to severe overfitting. When evaluated on the actual score script, performance collapsed: English actual delay spiked from 701ms up to 973ms, Hindi delay spiked from 595ms to 720ms, and ROC-AUC plummeted from ~0.91 to ~0.80[cite: 1]. Because of our strict "measure-then-decide" loop, we correctly identified the regression and rolled back to the robust, un-overfitted 16-feature model.

Methodology & Feature Engineering

The shipped model computes a 16-dimensional causal feature vector strictly from audio[0:pause_start]. Future frames and non-causal structural elements were explicitly excluded. A gradient-boosted tree classifier (GBDT, 150 shallow trees) was trained on English and Hindi pooled together, given that prosodic cues generally transfer across languages.

  • Pitch (F0) Dynamics: `f0_slope`, trailing level, and relative-to-turn mean. Falling pitch marks a completed declarative statement.
  • Energy Trajectory: Trailing level, slope, and energy decay across a 2.5s window.
  • Syllable Lengthening & Rhythm: `final_voiced_run_ratio` captures syntactical boundary lengthening. Speaking rate serves as structural context.
  • Vocal Fry Proxy: `zcr_last300ms` (Zero-Crossing Rate) detects vowel vs. consonant/fry endings[cite: 1].

The Failure Mode: ~35% of true End-of-Turns default to the 1.6s timeout[cite: 1]. Deep-dive error analysis (error_analysis.py) isolates this to short, single-pause confirmation questions (e.g., "...for seven, right?").

In turn en__015, for example, the final F0 slope was measured at +317.26[cite: 1]. The model learned that rising pitch equals a "hold" continuation. Because questions carry a rising terminal pitch boundary (H%), the model safely waits out the timer rather than risking a false interruption[cite: 1].

Strict Causality & Human vs. Agent Split

The assignment permits AI coding assistants but requires clear delineation of value added beyond generic generation. The pipeline was structured as follows:

AI Agent Execution & Extraction: Ran baseline validations, executed exploratory pitch/energy data framing, wrote the librosa extraction mathematics, and built the Grouped-CV hyperparameter grid search[cite: 1].

Human Architectural Strategy & Security: Defined the acoustic priorities (lengthening, vocal fry)[cite: 1]. More importantly, the human operator vetted and rejected causality-violating features generated by the agent (e.g., pause_duration_ms which leaked pause_end, and total_audio_duration which leaked future turn boundaries)[cite: 1]. Additionally, the human rejected an overfitted 52-feature expansion that degraded true held-out performance, rolling back to the robust 16-feature core model[cite: 1].
